Financial Statement Header Check

Review the header and identifying information of a financial statement — income statement, balance sheet, cash flow statement, or annual report — for the essentials that identify the statement and its period.

- The reporting entity is named (legal name, not just a logo or abbreviation)
- The statement type is clearly labelled (e.g., "Balance Sheet", "Statement of Operations")
- The reporting period is stated (as of date, or period start and end dates)
- The currency is stated or unambiguously identifiable
- The reporting scale is stated if amounts are not in whole units (e.g., "in thousands", "in millions")
- Prior-period comparison columns, if present, are clearly labelled with their own period
- Audited / unaudited / reviewed status is stated
- The preparer (auditor, CFO, or CEO) is identified where required
