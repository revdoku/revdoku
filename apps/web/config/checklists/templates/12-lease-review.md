Lease Review

Review a commercial or residential lease for the essentials, completeness, and common omissions.

- Landlord and tenant are both named with full legal names and addresses
- The leased premises are clearly described (address, unit / suite, square footage if commercial)
- Lease term is stated (start date, end date, or month-to-month)
- Rent amount, payment frequency, and due date are stated
- Security deposit amount, return conditions, and applicable deposit-cap law are addressed
- Permitted use of the premises is stated (residential only, or specific commercial use)
- Responsibility for utilities, maintenance, repairs, and insurance is allocated between landlord and tenant
- Rules on subletting, assignment, and alterations are stated
- Default, cure period, and termination rights are stated for both parties
- Renewal terms are addressed (auto-renewal, option to extend, or expires at end of term)
- Governing law / jurisdiction is specified
- Signature blocks are present for all parties with date
