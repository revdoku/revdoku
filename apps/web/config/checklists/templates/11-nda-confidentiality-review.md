NDA / Confidentiality Review

Review a non-disclosure / confidentiality agreement for the essentials and common omissions.

- Both parties (Disclosing Party and Receiving Party, or Mutual) are named with full legal names
- The effective date is present and not in the future
- The definition of "Confidential Information" is explicit — what qualifies and what does not
- Standard carve-outs from confidentiality are listed: information already public, independently developed, received from a third party without restriction, or required by law
- The term of confidentiality is stated (specific years or perpetual) with a clear start point
- A "return or destroy" clause covers what the Receiving Party must do with materials at termination
- The governing law / jurisdiction is specified
- Remedies for breach are stated (injunctive relief at minimum)
- Signature blocks are present for both parties with name, title, and date
- No placeholder text remains ("[Company]", "TBD", etc.)
