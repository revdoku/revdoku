MSA Review

Review a Master Service Agreement for the essentials that a framework contract needs.

- Both parties are named with full legal entities
- Effective date, initial term, and renewal terms are stated
- The relationship between the MSA and future SOWs / order forms is defined (precedence, incorporation)
- Fees, invoicing, and late-payment terms are stated
- Intellectual property ownership — background IP, foreground IP, and deliverable IP — is allocated
- Confidentiality obligations are stated, including term and carve-outs
- Warranties (service quality, non-infringement) are stated with clear scope
- Limitation of liability is present with a cap (either a fixed amount or a multiple of fees paid) and carve-outs
- Indemnification obligations are reciprocal or clearly allocated
- Termination rights are stated for both parties (for cause, for convenience, effect on active SOWs)
- Governing law, jurisdiction, and dispute resolution (arbitration vs. litigation) are specified
- Signature blocks are present with name, title, and date
