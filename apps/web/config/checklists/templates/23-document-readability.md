Document Readability

Review the legibility and structural integrity of a scanned or rendered document before content review.

- Every page is readable — no blurred, warped, or pixelated content
- No page is cropped mid-line or missing content at the edges
- Page orientation is consistent or intentionally mixed (e.g., landscape table pages in an otherwise-portrait document)
- No black bars, redaction boxes, or scanner artifacts obscure content that should be visible
- Pages appear in the correct order (page numbers, if present, are sequential)
- No duplicate pages unless intentional (e.g., a cover sheet repeated)
- No missing pages in the middle (gaps in page numbering, references to content not present)
- Text is selectable / OCR-recognized where the document is supposed to be digital-native
- Tables, charts, and figures render cleanly (no misaligned columns, broken lines, truncated legends)
- Headers, footers, and watermarks do not obscure body content
