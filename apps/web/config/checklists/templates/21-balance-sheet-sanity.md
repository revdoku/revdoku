Balance Sheet Sanity

Review a balance sheet for basic internal consistency — the accounting equation holds, signs are correct, and no major line items are missing.

- Total assets equals total liabilities plus total equity (the fundamental accounting equation)
- Current assets subtotal equals the sum of listed current-asset line items
- Non-current (long-term) assets subtotal equals the sum of listed non-current-asset line items
- Total assets equals current assets plus non-current assets
- Current liabilities subtotal equals the sum of listed current-liability line items
- Non-current liabilities subtotal equals the sum of listed non-current-liability line items
- Total liabilities equals current plus non-current liabilities
- Total equity equals the sum of its listed components (share capital, retained earnings, reserves, etc.)
- No asset line item is negative unless it is a contra-asset explicitly labelled as such (accumulated depreciation, allowance for doubtful accounts)
- No liability or equity line item is negative unless it is a contra account or a loss position explicitly labelled
- Prior-period comparison columns, if present, also balance independently
- All amounts are shown to a consistent decimal place and in a single currency
