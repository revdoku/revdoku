Report Structure Check

Review the structural elements of a multi-section report or white paper for completeness and navigability.

- Title page shows title, author(s), organization, and date
- Table of contents is present with page numbers
- Every TOC entry corresponds to an actual section in the report
- Every section in the report appears in the TOC (no orphan sections)
- Executive summary or abstract is present near the beginning
- Section headers follow a consistent hierarchy (e.g., 1, 1.1, 1.1.1 — no skipped levels)
- Page numbers are present and sequential
- Figures and tables are numbered and captioned
- In-text references to figures, tables, or sections resolve to something that exists in the document
- References / bibliography section is present if the document cites external sources
- Every in-text citation has a corresponding entry in the references section, and vice versa
- Conclusion or summary section is present at the end
